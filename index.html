Persona Analysis based on TripAdvisor

Final-year Project, Nov. 2017 to Present

At Beijing University of Posts & Telecommunications

The aim of this project is to build a persona analysis model and visualize the analysis results. A persona is a ficional character created to represent a user type that might use a site, brand, or product in a similiar way. Marketers may use personas together with market segmentation, where the persona are constructed to be representative of specific segments. Thus, the use of personas offers benefits in product development.

Therefore, it is meaningful to do some research work on persona analysis. In this project, I have four main tasks : 1. Collect users' comments from website; 2. Train a persona analysis model; 3. Apply the model into real analysis work; 4. Visualize the analysis results.

Space-time Data Analysis and Visualization - (Sept. 2016 to Mar. 2017)

Student Research Intern at Institute of Software, Chinese Academy of Science

In this project, my main job was to analyze sensor data to find anomalous patterns. In so doing, I became more proficient at typical machine learning algorithms such as k-means, k-nearest neighbor (NN), and Gaussian distribution. For instance, while looking for anomalies in our sensor data, I found it more effective to select normal points as Gaussian kernels with their variances determined by the sparsity of the neighbor normal points than to directly adopt a k-means algorithm. In addition, for optimization, I divided the data into multiple batches and distributed the analysis among multiple processes, successfully reducing the running time from tens of hours to three hours.

Research on Software Trustworthiness Evaluation - (Sept. 2015 to May 2016)

Student Research Intern at School of Software Engineering, BUPT

In this project, I worked on a project analyzing and evaluating software trustworthiness. We proposed to research quantitative evaluations based on the final product. The first thing I did was to define the trustworthy attributes of a software product, both internally and externally. According to multi-criteria complex decision-making scenarios, we chose the AHP method and then decomposed the evaluation problem into a hierarchy of more easily comprehended sub-problems, each of which could be analyzed independently. Our paper on this quantitative evaluation model of software trustworthiness was accepted in January 2017.
